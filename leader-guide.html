Tonight's Flow — Leader Coaching Notes
6:30 – 6:50
Connection Time
20 min
Leader Posture Be ON before anyone walks in. Doors open, food ready, music playing. You set the tone — if you're relaxed and warm, they will be.
Welcome at the Door Greet everyone by name. If you don't know their name, introduce yourself first. People feel the difference between being greeted and being processed.
Icebreaker: —
Kick Off Icebreaker at 6:35 Call the room to attention briefly. Keep it light and fun. Don't force it — model being open yourself and others will follow. Aim for everyone to speak at least once.
6:50 – 7:05
Directional Teaching
15 min
Stay Under 15 Minutes This is a directional moment, not a full sermon. A hook story → scripture → one clear takeaway. If you're going past 7:05, you're going too long.
Teach to the Takeaway Everything in your teaching should point to the one takeaway. Say it clearly. Say it again. Make it sticky.
Tonight's takeaway: —
Vision Moment (30 sec) Somewhere in the teaching, drop a vision seed: "We're building something special in St. Pete. This is what it looks like..." Keep it brief, not preachy.
7:05 – 7:45
Table Discussion
40 min
Don't Answer First Ask the question and let silence breathe. Count to 10 internally if you need to. The first person to speak models the depth of conversation for everyone else — make them be the bravest.
Facilitation Over Talking Your job is to draw people out, not impress them. Ask follow-ups: "Say more about that." "Has anyone else experienced that?" "What made that hard for you?"
Watch the Room Who's quiet? Who wants to say something but isn't sure? Pull them in gently: "Hey, [name], what do you think?" Never put them on the spot — just open a door.
Time Check at 7:30 You need 15 minutes for the ownership moment. At 7:30, start wrapping the discussion. "Let's close on one last thought..."
7:45 – 8:00
Ownership Moment
15 min
Name the Next Step Clearly Don't be vague. People can't respond to "get more involved." They CAN respond to "We're starting a men's group — who's in?"
Tonight's CTA: —
Ask for a Response in the Room "Who's in? Raise your hand." OR "If you're open to it, come see me before you leave." Create a moment of real commitment, not just intention.
Close With Energy Pray over the room, specifically. Name what God is doing. Cast vision. People leave charged, not just informed.

Leader Principles — Always True
🔥
Connection beats content. If someone leaves feeling seen and known, the night worked — regardless of how the teaching went.
🎯
One clear next step. Don't give people three options. Give them one. Make it easy to say yes.
📱
Follow up within 24 hours. Every new person gets a personal text the next day. Every time. No exceptions.
👀
Watch for the next leader. Someone in the room tonight could run their own group in 6 months. Identify them. Invest in them.
🙏
Start with prayer, lead from spirit. Come prepared, but stay responsive. The Holy Spirit moves in the room — don't be so locked in your plan you miss it.
